I'm choosing "The Five Dysfunctions of a Team" by Patrick Lencioni. 


> "Written as a business fable, which makes it very readable. Argues that teams fail because of five cascading problems: absence of trust, fear of conflict, lack of commitment, avoidance of accountability, and inattention to results. Each dysfunction feeds the next, so trust is the foundation everything else rests on. Practically very applicable to software teams. What does trust mean to you? What is possible in a high trust environment versus a low trust environment?"
